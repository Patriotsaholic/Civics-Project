Propaganda Techniques Identification Guide

1. Testimonial

Slide 3

How it works: Uses Phil Perry (NFL Analyst) endorsement to lend credibility and authority to Drake Maye's MVP candidacy. By associating Maye with an experienced NFL analyst, it makes him seem more legitimate and worthy. Exploits our tendency to trust expert opinions and authority figures.

2. Bandwagon

Slide 4

How it works: Informs that "Rob Grokowski and Julian Edelman have stated Drake Maye has their vote" and suggests many supporters already back Maye, creating social pressure and fear of missing out. Makes voters feel like they'd be going against popular consensus if they don't vote for him. Appeals to our desire to be part of the winning side.

3. Name Calling

Slide 5

How it works: Attacks other MVP candidates with negative labels like "overrated," "pretenders," and "stat-chasers" while calling their supporters "blind and biased." Creates emotional responses that make people want to distance themselves from the opposition and avoid being associated with these negative terms.

4. Glittering Generalities

Slide 6

How it works: Uses vague, emotionally powerful words like "excellence," "destiny," "honor," "courage," and "greatness" without specific meaning or evidence. These words trigger positive emotions and patriotic feelings without making concrete, verifiable claims about Maye's actual performance.

5. Card Stacking

Slide 7

How it works: Presents only selective statistics that favor Drake Maye (strong performances, game winning drives) while avoiding mention of areas where other candidates might lead or where he's still developing as a rookie. This one sided presentation manipulates perception by hiding less favorable data or context.

6. Plain Folks Appeal

Slide 8

How it works: Portrays Drake Maye as relatable and down to earth by showing support from everyday working-class people and emphasizing his humble characteristics. Makes him seem like "one of us" rather than an elite athlete, creating emotional connection and trust with regular voters.
